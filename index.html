The Hound of the Baskervilles


New learnt today:

  1. Tone: In order to judge the tone of a passage it is important to identify the central feeling of the narrator by reading the entire passage carefully and understand his/her concern from his/her perspective.
  2. Mood: The mood of a literary passage is made evident by the setting and language the author uses for a certain story.
  3. Claim and Evidence: Claims are statements that support the thesis but are not facts. Because a claim is not a fact, it must be supported by evidence. Evidence is factual information that proves a claim is correct.

New Words we learnt today:

  1. Ordeal: a very unpleasant and prolonged experience
  2. Surmise: suppose that something is true without having evidence to confirm it/guess/conjecture
  3. Moor: a tract of open uncultivated upland, typically covered with heather.
  4. Stride: a long, decisive step.
  5. Tiptoe: walk quietly and carefully with one's heels raised and one's weight on the balls of the feet
  6. Ambush: a surprise attack by people lying in wait in a concealed position.
  7. Latticed: decorated with or in the form of a lattice.
  8. Stooped: (of a person) having the head and shoulders habitually bent forwards.
  9. Stunted: having been prevented from growing or developing properly.
  10. Baronet: a man who has the lowest title of honour that can be given in the UK , below a baron but above a knight, and given from father to son.
  11. Distraught: very worried and upset.
  12. Scuffling: move in a hurried , confused, or awkward way, making a rustling or shuffling sound.
  13. Tors: a hill or rocky peak.
  14. Sluggish: slow-moving or inactive
  15. Serrated: having or denoting a jagged edge; sawlike.
  16. Bristling: aggressively brisk or tense
  17. Silver-spangled: decorated with small pieces of shiny metal or plastic
  18. Inexorably: in a way that is impossible to stop or prevent.
  19. Crouching: adopt a position where the knees are bent and the upper body is brought forward and down, typically in order to avoid detection or to defend oneself.
